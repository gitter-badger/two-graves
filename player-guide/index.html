Welcome, and thank you for your interest in the Two Graves adventure path! This adventure will take you through the nation of Seorsus, a paragon of the virtuous, civilized world. From its highest peaks to its deepest caverns, you will become embroiled in the nation’s mysterious past, the lives of its people, and the shaping of its future.

In this guide you will find information about Seorsus and its major settlments that any individual who lives in or has briefly researched the nation would know. Feel free to use these sections to help build your characters and integrate them into the world.

Modern Life in Seorsus

In the heart of the valley of Multae— protected by the forboding Devil’s Tail Mountains to the north and the unfathomable Great Rift to the south— Seorsus echoes with a clarion call of opportunity. Low, rolling hills stretch across the landscape, and lush green forests intermingle with networks of towns and villages. Occasionally, gleaming cities sit at the center of these networks, marking the terrain with intricate webs of road that connect settlements for miles around. Along the foothills, frontier towns dot lands carved with snow-melt rivers that flow from the peaks above down into the Clearlake.

The abundance of the valley may provide, but ultimately the people of Seorsus are its lifeblood. Most citizens live and work in ostensibly simple villages. There they develop arable land and pull forth riches from the valley’s mines. Their harvests flow inward toward the cities of the nation, where they supply the insatiable craftsmen and merchants who give Seorsus its reputation as an economic and cultural superpower. In return, the inner settlements provide ample public works, education and infrastructure which allow the outland residents to specialize and thrive. All the while, brave and resourceful frontiersmen pave the way for others to follow in their footsteps and expand the glory of the nation.

A Brief History of the Nation

According to national history, centuries ago the valley of Multae was long uninhabited and widely thought to be a cursed land. Determined to break ground for civilization where all others had failed, Abadaran crusaders embarked on a mission to colonize the valley. They swore a holy oath to build a shining metropolis that would last for centuries to come. From their effort and toil the nation of Seorsus was born. Spurred on by their success, they created the organization known as the Founders and dedicated it to sharing Seorsus’s glory with the world. To that end, they established a means of constructing Thresholds—magical gates used to travel to and from Seorsus. Immigrants to the new nation were slow to come at first, but they did arrive. And as they arrived, they started successful new lives in the bountiful valley. Word spread, and the population of Seorsus grew exponentially as people from all over the continent came to investigate this new wonder and found their expectations exceeded. It was not long before the fledgling nation became the hub of luxury, trade, and travel that it is today.

The Government of Seorsus

Outwardly, the government of Seorsus is composed of three parts: a Senate, an Emperor and District Representatives. Every four years, citizens elect District Representatives to speak for their regions' interests in government affairs. While they lack individual power, as a collective the District Representatives possess great strength, and most take immense pride in their role. They elect the Emperor from their ranks and may even vote to forcibly remove Senators from office for life. Consequently, even the most powerful officials respect District Representatives as the voice of the people.

The current Emperor, Marshall Blackwood, was elected five years ago. Thus far, his term has been mostly uneventful. Emperor Blackwood has not needed to wield his power to veto any major proposals, resolve clutch ties in the Senate, or directly command the National Police Force. Popular opinion is that he will choose to hold a twenty year term like most of his predecessors, though no law requires him to do so.

While the Emperor and District Representatives are elected officials, each of the nation’s eminent temples are granted a number of Senate seats corresponding to their influence. Senators, once inaugurated, occupy their positions for life, unless deposed by the District Representatives. Because Senators wield mighty power such as ratification of laws and calling for immediate re-election of the Emperor, the temples deliberate carefully when selecting leaders to become Senators. As a result, most Senators are level-headed and keenly aware that they must walk the line between the interests of their temples, the people, and the nation at large.

Who’s in the Senate?

A temple receives a number of Senate positions corresponding with its influence in the nation, be it influence through size of congregation, wealth, or other means. Currently, the temple of Abadar is allocated eight senate seats. Shelyn holds three, and Sarenrae and Cayden Cailean each hold two. Pharasma, Iomedae, and Erastil are all represented, but each holds only one seat.

In practice, of course, the government is much more than just the sum of its leadership. Of the various government agencies and divisions, the largest and most publicly visible are the National Police Force, the Department of Customs and Immigration, and the Public Works Agency. The National Police Force is charged with maintaining peace and safety throughout the nation, and is the closest thing Seorsus has to a military force. The current head of police, Nigel Croft, continues to uphold the organization’s excellent reputation. Although he may claim that they are chronicly understaffed and underfunded, Seorsus is an incredibly safe country. The Department of Customs and Immigration levying taxes on exported goods, and also control and maintain the magical gates that lead to Seorsus. The Public Works Agency builds and maintains infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and waterways. It also settles disputes over public lands, most frequently regarding the ancient relics scattered about the country.

Religion in Seorsus

Although the nation is not aligned with any one deity, Seorsus’s roots in the Founders' religious crusade are still apparent to this day. The nation’s temples play a huge role in the citizens' day-to-day lives. The temples act most directly by guiding the course of the nation through the Senators, but also on a local scale and in unseen ways as their values and agendas bleed into the surrounding culture.

The temple of Abadar has maintained major power in the nation, and from the gilded halls of The First Capitol in Regunt it oversees much of the nation’s financial and legal well-being. Shelyn and Cayden Cailean both have large followings in Oblectant. Their temples, Couatl’s Voice and The Best Vault, draw tourists and worshippers from far and wide with the promise of first rate entertainment and culture.

Mystery of the Shrines

Scattered around the landscape of Seorsus are mysterious shrines. The shrines wield various potent magics, from healing, to increased strength and speed, to the ability to breath underwater for a time. Although the purpose and origin of these shrines is contested fact, access to them is highly valued, and many groups vie for stewardship of these relics.

Iomedae’s temple is the Praesidium in Regunt, and much of the National Police Force are devotees. Sarenrae, Pharasma, and Erastil enjoy large popular support from all around the nation for their public services and good works, but they do not maintain large shrines or temples in any one location. Many other faiths have worshippers in Seorsus, and although they do not hold prominent political power, Desna, Gozreh, Calistria, and Irori all have noteworthy followings around the country. Meanwhile, abroad, the Founders work tirelessly to spread the gospel of civilization’s crowning achievements, and in their triumph bring ever new and excited souls to join as citizens.

The Central Cities

The Central Cities are the largest urban centers in Seorsus. They represent the peak of culture and progress in the nation, and each of them has developed their own unique customs and specialties over time.

Common Government Resources

You can expect most government agencies that deal with infrastructure will have representation in all large settlements, although they may not be specifically called out in these sections. This includes, but is not limited to, divisions such as the National Police Force, Public Works Agency, and other servants of the common good.

Oblectant, City of Arts

Brimming with life and activity, the city of Oblectant is the veritable pulse of Seorsus. Artists and entertainers from all walks of life flock to join in Oblectant’s city’s cultural melting pot and experience the wealth the city has to offer. Anywhere is a stage, and revelry spills out into the streets, infusing the city with a constant state of festivity. Each day could bring new celebrations replete with performances and displays unlike any seen before it. As a result, Oblectant is a hot spot for tourists from around the world, and their presence ensures that there is plenty of gold to keep the entertainers at their antics.

Officials long gave up on imposing bureaucracy upon the citizens, and now simply seek to ensure that the city runs smoothly from one performance to the next. The last vestige of control the government maintains over the celebrations is the Festival Planning Committee, which stages city-wide festivals, schedules access to large venues such as Lavender Fields, and provides publicity for ambitious performers. The National Police Force patrols the streets, keeping the city safe for residents and tourists alike, but their presence is understated and does not impinge on the Oblectant’s carefree atmosphere. Petty crime is uncommon, and offenders are swiftly swept to the nearest police station or courthouse for judgment.

Despite the apparent laissez-faire attitude of the government, Oblectant is extremely well-maintained. Buildings of sturdy wood and cut stone stand strong against the never-ending flow of residents, and paved streets make for easy transport around the city. Lights both magical and mundane line the streets and buildings, vying for the attention of passersby. Even with messy tourists who care not for the city’s cleanliness, strong sewers and Oblectant’s famed trash removal kobolds sweep away the filth and leave the city ready for the festivities to continue.

Places of Interest
Dragon’s Teeth

Rows of enormous amethyst crystals jutting from the entrance give Oblectant’s premier amphitheater it’s name. Although its appearance and sublime acoustics would suggest a man-made structure, Dragon’s Teeth is a naturally occurring geode phenomenon. Performing even once in the Dragon’s Teeth is the goal of many artists, and competition is fierce. The revered Auditioners are the final elite arbiters of who will be graced by the stage.

Couatl’s Voice

Vivid botanical gardens and serene marble galleries compose the nation’s premier temple of Shelyn. The temple is built on the edge of the city and situated near the Dragon’s Teeth so that the clergy may more easily tend to the amphitheater. Compared to the intensity of the city, Couatl’s Voice is a sanctuary for those who wish to reflect upon the spiritual impact of the arts.

Bell University

As one of the world’s most prestigious schools for the fine arts, Bell University is a central location for both prospective students and tourists alike. Although its intimidating spires, sharply slanted rooftops, and aged stone walls cast an imposing shadow, they belie the mood within. Its campus buzzes jovially at all hours as some of the greatest creative geniuses from around the globe hone their skills.

Lavender Fields

Named for the carpet of small purple flowers that blooms in the spring, the Lavender Fields is an expansive, open-air park and a prime location for many public events. Complete with several small venues for performances as well as a large stadium, the Lavender Fields boast ample space and seating for all visitors. Whether it be music, sports, or festivals, an event in the Lavender Fields is sure to be lively and draw a large crowd.

Capo Bridge

Spanning the Limen River and joining the east and west of Oblectant together, the Capo Bridge is more than just an important piece of infrastructure. Many bits of Oblectant culture have incorporated the Capo Bridge. Long now has it been tradition to toss flowers into the river below for good luck, and locals swear that lovers who watch fireworks from the bridge will be blessed with happiness. More practically, it’s also a natural choke point for traffic through the city. Its wide stone path is filled with street performers, each trying to take advantage of their captive audiences.

The Best Vault

A large stone carving of a frothing mug adorns the facade of Oblectant’s favorite temple to the god of drinking and merriment. This multi-story wood and stone building radiates a warmth and festivity that can be felt from blocks away. The temple hosts a stage and doubles as both a comedy house and theater for celebrations of bravery. To the confusion of tourists and the amusement of locals, the interior of the temple is themed as a bank.

Plaza District

Surrounding Oblectant’s central plazas, this bustling district is a hot spot for tourism and revelry. Although most come to the district to be at the center of the city’s festivals, competition between enterprises to garner attention has made the district a spectacle in its own right. Themed businesses, wild street performers, and garish decorations all lend a hand in creating the district’s unique atmosphere.

College District

Fed by the youthful, creative energies of Bell University, the College District is the premiere place for mingling and scouting rising talent in the city. Located on the western side of the Capo Bridge, this region is not as upscale as some parts of Oblectant, but its inhabitants are jovial and create a welcoming atmosphere. Collaboration and competition go hand-in-hand here, and friendly rivalries often erupt into spontaneous performances in the pursuit of great art.

Garden District

Mostly separated from the hustle and bustle of the city, the Garden District provides a serene getaway in the northeast of Oblectant for those who can afford it. Residents enjoy beautiful courtyards, spotless streets, and exclusive close access to iconic locations such as Dragon’s Teeth and Couatl’s Voice.

Barrelbottom District

To call the Barrelbottom District a slum would be disingenuous, but compared to the rest of Oblectant this southwestern district falls behind in upkeep and cleanliness. Tenement housing is plentiful, and in place of high caliber pursuits the residents of Barrelbottom District excel in vice entertainment. Seedy bars and brothels adorn the streets, and the venues host bawdy and burlesque performances too vulgar for popular taste.

Warehouse District

Tucked away in the less-noticeable southeastern portion of the city, the Warehouse District houses offices and storage for government agencies such as the National Police Force and Festival Planning Committee.

Masters District

Many master craftsmen who outfit Oblectant with superior quality instruments and supplies base their workshops in the Masters District. These great makers demand a particular environment for their work, and the district has molded to reflect their tastes. Simple, refined architecture and narrow cobbled streets give the district a historic atmosphere, and even the wildest of locals calm themselves out of respect when in the area. The district lies near the southern road to Crent, a fact which many of the masters here take advantage of to secure access to incoming materials.

Crent, City of Markets

The sound of roaring forges and workshops blends with the clink of coins in the vibrant market city of Crent. Known for its unparalleled selection of masterwork and exotic goods, Crent is a haven for craftsmen. The best of the best travel here to study with other masters from all around. The result is a crucible of rivalries and collaborations which drive the innovations of these ambitious creators ever forward. This work and success have brought incredible renown to both Crent and the nation as a whole, and wealth naturally flows to the city in the form of hardened merchants and more casual buyers.

The siren call of riches and skill brings along with it much more strict government oversight. Left to their own devices, foreign merchants would surely bleed the country dry. In response, agencies such as the Department of Customs and Immigration and the Department of Labor keep close watch on the conditions of the city. Given the amount of coin that flows through the streets, the National Police Force are omnipresent in Crent, ensuring that the city retains its name as a safe and reputable haven for trade.

The master craftsmen themselves are no pushovers either. Crafting guilds are strictly forbidden both in a legal sense and a communal one. Here, each individual crafter vies for the reputation of her own name. Consequently, mutual respect—even among the most obvious of rivals—is paramount and palpable in the transactions that take place here.

To feed the high demand of buyers, Crent has built itself up around a system of attractive and efficient roads and neighborhoods. No space is wasted in thoroughfares large or small, as every bit of available wall space is transformed into a storefront or workshop. Sturdy stone buildings resist wear from unending shoppers, but also from nature’s harshness and the occasional workshop fire. Goods transit smoothly through the city’s wide, paved streets—their navigation made easy by numerous road markers. At night, hanging iron lamps illuminate the city’s famous twenty-four hour markets.

Places of Interest
Grand Bazaar

Thousands of shoppers browse the rows of tents that fill this enormous open-air market. As one of Crent’s internationally iconic features, the Grand Bazaar is home to all sorts of novelties and unique items. Although locally regarded as more for show than function, finding real treasures nestled in the wares here is not unheard of.

House of Commerce

Dozens of imposing wooden panels line the walls of this large forum, each filled with statistics and predictions for various market goods. Harried merchant apprentices hustle along the walls with tablets and chalk in an unending effort to keep the boards up to date. Meanwhile, cold-faced merchants and crafters eye the ever changing boards like vipers ready to strike.

Nascereli

Nascereli is Seorsus’s largest home of elves. Here the elves have fostered a small forest grove and made their homes in its trunks and boughs. Elves, half-elves, and fey races are openly welcomed, but most others find themselves roughly tolerated as permanent residents.

Khurulag

Not to be outdone by the forest of Nascereli or the cut stone of Crent itself, the dwarves constructed a miniature mountainscape out of boulders from the nation’s mines. Now complete with carved homes and bold designs, Khurulag is a colony in its own right. Although legally no citizen may be forbidden residence here, most non-dwarves find Khurulag’s stony, cramped interior unwelcoming.

Lakeside Harbor

Cargo boats fill the numerous docks of this large harbor on the Clearlake. Much of Crent’s imports and exports pass through this harbor, and deckhands work feverishly to keep the flow of goods moving.

Limen River Customs

Owned by the Department of Customs and Immigration, this customs house sits on an island in the Limen River just before the river passes through the city. Ships queue up to have their cargo inspected and their paperwork approved by the grim-faced and serious officials who work here.

The Heights

Perched atop the highest hill of Crent’s southwest lakefront, The Heights houses the wealthiest citizens and dignitaries of the city. A stunning lakeside vista complements the opulent estates here, and the city’s most reputable envoys traverse the boulevards on business for their employers.

Coin District

The central Coin District is home to the majority of Crent’s storefronts and markets and is the city’s largest and most famous district. From small one-man shops to the Grand Bazaar itself, the Coin District encompasses trade of all sizes and types.

Workshop District

Strategically located between the Lakeside Harbor to the south and the storefronts of Coin District to the north, the Workshop District houses makers who require larger, more complex facilities. The sounds of creation clamor forth from the district’s studios and forges as each crafter develops his next masterpiece.

North District

North District provides accommodation for residents who do not live in their stores or workshops and visitors who choose not to pay the premiums of inns in the Coin District. Although they are not the gilded estates of The Heights, the homes and buildings in this district are by no means in shambles. Even the simplest housing in Crent reflects the city’s wealth and high standards of living.

Operations District

Home to the offices of information brokers, merchant guild members, and government officials, the southeastern Operations District hosts the unseen web of data and law that keeps the city running. Through word of mouth and fleet couriers, information and commands rush from this district to recipients within and beyond Crent’s borders.

Regunt, City of Laws

At the heart of Seorsus is the nation’s crown jewel: the capital city of Regunt. Towering, gleaming buildings of marble and polished stone strike a grand vista for those who first lay eyes upon the city. Orderly grids of streets and avenues bustle with activity, each individual doing his part to propel the nation towards progress. At night, although the streets quiet to dim illumination by heavy iron lamps, the work of the city does not cease. Buildings big and small come to life as interior lights shine through their windows, offering tantalizing hints at the ongoings within.

From first sight to last, Regunt whisks away newcomers and locals alike into an embrace of structure and control that seems almost caring. The city runs like clockwork, with each inhabitant and agency fulfilling a purpose in a timely and efficient manner. From the lowliest courier to the highest seats of government, every citizen of Regunt is devoted fully to their cause. To do otherwise would be to invite certain scorn and disdain from one’s peers.

Just as each citizen is devoted to their tasks, so is the city itself devoted to the law. Law is born and dies in Regunt. Every agency houses its highest offices here in this locus of government power. Yet despite the proximity of government control, the city does not quake under the tyranny of authoritarianism. That the government serves its people is a well respected fact, and it is commonly understood that Regunt only functions at the willing compliance of its citizens.

Places of Interest
Capitol Building

A quartet of obelisks surround this domed building which towers above the inner city. With architectural adornments and intricacies that border on ostentatiousness, the seat of Seorsus’s governmental power serves as a constant reminder of the nation’s political and economic mastery. Here the Senate holds council with the Emperor, and the majority of government agencies also keep their headquarters within.

Grand Threshold

A gilded quarter-sphere in the north of the city contains the divine masterpiece through which travel using the Thresholds is made possible. Heavily staffed by the National Police Force and the Department of Customs and Immigration, hundreds of travelers are processed daily here. The quarter-sphere opens to a dazzling view of the Capitol Building, imposing the nation’s crown jewel as the first and last thing journeyers see during their time in Seorsus.

The First Capitol

Originally the capitol building during the nation’s infancy, Abadar’s temple has since returned to its primary function. Although it may not have the same prestige it once did, the temple has certainly not degraded in splendor. From within the church’s golden halls the clergy works tirelessly to bring the vision of their forebears, the Founders, to life.

Mercantile Guildhall

Just north of the docks and warehouses on the Clearlake are a series of large, utilitarian guildhalls. Collected within are offices of the various merchant guilds that do business in Seorsus. Although the guilds would almost certainly prefer to be headquartered in Crent, the law requires that they be located here in Regunt. The cunning merchants have made the best of their situation, though, taking every opportunity to endlessly lobby in the interest of their guilds.

Seorsus University of Law and Business

In the east of the city is the Seorsus University of Law and Business, the incubator of Regunt’s commercial and political genius. It is nigh unheard of that prominent figures do not have some tie to the university, making it the prime entry point to the upper echelons of Seorsus’s political economy. As a result, its student body does not just comprise promising, untested politicians and merchants, but also veterans and experts from other parts of the country looking to network and advance their positions.

Knight Library

Adjacent to the Seorsus University of Law and Business rises the nation’s premier repository of knowledge. From modern research journals to ancient folklore, the Knight Library provides access to an enormous wealth of information at no cost. The Stewards of the library work tirelessly to expand and preserve the collection, and are often seen moving like ghosts through the book stacks.

Praesidium

The headquarters of the National Police Force is a sharp building supported by thick columns of polished stone which stand vigil along its perimeter. The majority of citizens will only ever enter the temple of Iomedae and the public offices within the compound. It is well known, however, that the Praesidium also conceals a well-guarded network of armories, archives, and barracks.

Imperial Residence

Lush, tall gardens and a host of guards keep this gleaming marble estate private and safe. Despite its seclusion from the public eye, even here the Emperor cannot escape the responsibilities of his position. A direct view to the Capitol Building cuts through the manor’s grounds, in keeping with a law that decrees the Emperor’s bed must always face the capitol, such that his duties will be his last sight before retiring and his first upon waking.

Commuter’s District

Commuter’s District caters to citizens who need or choose to spend their time between Regunt and other settlements. The area features modest, efficient housing and a large number of inns in the northwest of the city with convenient access to the major roads between Regunt, Crent, and Oblectant.

Uptown District

Home to many of Regunt’s powerful mercantile and political elite, the Uptown District features lavish estates with secluded vistas onto the Clearlake. Tactically located in the southeast of the city near hotbeds of influence such as the Principal District, Seorsus University of Law and Business, and Commercial District, soirees and galas featuring the nation’s most renowned often take place here.

Principal District

The largest and most central district in the city, the Principal District contains the majority of Regunt’s government buildings and centers of religious worship. Neat and orderly even under the weight of the thousands working here, this district could almost be considered a settlement of its own within the city. Businesses of all types have been built to support the district’s employees, ensuring they are well provided for as they toil for the good of the nation.

Main Plaza

The northern region of the city is filled with open gardens and other areas for public gatherings. The Main Plaza lies in contrast with the purposeful feeling that pervades the rest of Regunt, providing residents and visitors with an escape from unending the rush of the city.

Commercial District

This district lies on Regunt’s southern border with the Clearlake and houses the city’s harbors, markets, and enterprises like the Mercantile Guildhall. Busy at all times of day and night, goods endlessly flow through wide streets for sale, storage, and evaluation.

Races in Seorsus

Commonalities Across Races

Due to the high commercial and entertainment specialties of Seorsus, it draws craftsman and tourists from all over the world. Unless described otherwise, you can assume that almost any race can be represented as either a craftsman or a tourist.

Futhermore, the nation of Krig does not discriminate against any individual so long as she can carry her weight in war. Krigerian bodyguards, hunters, policemen, and members of any other profession involving martial prowess can easily find employment in Seorsus regardless of their racial background.

Humans

Despite the generally high levels of diversity in the country, humans are the dominant race in the Central Cities and in the nation at large. Humans native to Seorsus are represented in all professions, and tend to hold the majority of influential positions in both the economic and political spheres. They can be found in every major city and in almost every outlying area of the country.

Humans hailing from other parts of Norolba tend to come to Seorsus for more specialized reasons. Farmers, clerics on pilgrimage, and visiting aristocrats from Folda are all common sights. The goat-riding Vuori from the peaks of Orgiluud are more rare, but sometimes come down to the valley for supplies or to seek lesser, or perhaps even greater, solitude than they find among their people in the mountains. The exotic Hariq from neighboring Mughim are often employed as guides and laborers, although some leave their homes to make a living as performers in Oblectant. Humans from Alberi are significantly more rare, but occasionally a prodigy from the forest’s mage academy or a wayward druid will make an appearance.

Dwarves

Seorsus is ripe with rich underground resources, and as a result dwarves are frequently drawn here. Most dwarves hail from the mountains in Orgiluud where they once lived with their fellow bretheren of the Dwarfmother. Once here, they tend to prefer life in the large network of dwarven homes that have been built under the earth. Although most dwarves will be living in the outlying areas of the nation, many certainly do find their way to the Central Cities.

Many dwarves will find Crent to their liking, as the ever-open markets and vendors offer a great opportunity to sell wares and find rare materials. Some dwarves with an eye for government or leadership settle in Regunt and take a post in a guild or legal office. Dwarves in the legal realm tend to put their strong racial loyalties to use by defending the interests of other dwarves. Consequently, many find their way into positions as District Representatives. Dwarves rarely tend to have the patience for as wild a city as Oblectant.

Elves

Elves are rarely found in Seorsus outside of the Central Cities. Drawn by the wealth of craftsmanship and artistic talent, elves come to display their own prowess and compare their works to other master artisans'. Because of this, most elves make their home in the de-facto elven district in Crent. There they can live in a familiar manner while still reaping the advantages of the city. Elves almost never bother with the political and mercantile business that takes place in Regunt, but are frequently drawn to the lively music and performances in Oblectant.

Most of the elves living in Seorsus hail from from Alberi, and those that are not craftsmen are most often artists, performers, members of Alberi's mage’s academy, druids, rangers, or clerics of Shelyn.

Gnomes

The constant festivites and general excitement of Oblectant are perfectly suited to the typical gnome’s demeanor. While some rise to great heights in famous troupes, most gnomes find great pleasure in being street performers and vendors. Gnomes with an eye for business or a craving for the excitement of commercial enterprises will sometimes venture into Crent to try their luck. Regunt is far too boring and rigid for the majority of gnomes, and they are rarely seen there.

Halflings

Halflings can be found in great numbers all over the country, but they dovetail with particular ease into the hubbub and excitement of the Central Cities. Artisans, shopkeepers, performers, and even occasionally politicians, a Seorsus halfling’s upbeat and amiable attitude is a formidable asset.

Halflings coming from Folda are often members of the merchant class seeking potential business partners. The rural, hill-giant loving halflings from Orgiluud are more likely to become farmers in the outskirts of the nation.

Half-Elves

Half-elves have flourished in Seorsus, especially in the Central Cities where diversity is practically omnipresent. They face next to no discrimination for their mixed origin, and many travel from distant lands to come here. Their integration has been so successful, in fact, that half-elves are now the second most populous race in Seorsus. Here, their dual cultures play to their advantage, and they are well suited to any role. However, many heed the call of their elven side and find themselves as either artisans or performers in Oblectant.

Half-Orcs

Although diversity is welcomed in the Central Cities, brutishness and crassness are not. Although not hated or feared by any means, most half-orcs simply do not fit into a metropolitan lifestyle, and so choose instead to live in the outlying towns and villages of the nation. Those who do choose to live in the cities tend to make their living as bodyguards for wealthy politicians or merchants, or as a part of the National Police Force.

Classes in Seorsus

Barbarians

The civilized and well-kept lands of Seorsus are inhospitable to barbarian tribes. Barbarism and brutishness are frowned upon universally across the country, and many barbarians would find it a challenge to be welcomed or to fit in. As there are no naturally occurring tribes in the valley, most barbarians trace their origins elsewhere in Norolba. In particular, barbarians thrive in the blazing sands of Mughim and in the gladiatorial pits of Krig.

In Mughim, fire-blooded Hariq are the warriors of nomadic tribes who roam the desert, never staying in one place for too long. The Hariq’s nomadism is driven by their perceived duty to safeguard their home and its culture. Despite their traditionalism and battlelust, many Hariq are pragmatists and some fund their tribe’s goals by working as mercenaries. Others may be outcasts or glory seekers looking to start a new life in a land different from what they have known.

Krigerian barbarians frequently rise to great ranks in the warrior culture of their homeland. There, where power and determination are revered, barbarians can live akin to nobility. However, feats of greatness must be constantly proven, and one can only rise so high without a worthy tale of one’s own. To this end, many leave Krig to travel the continent in search of fame and honor. But for each glorified warrior, there are many defeated ones in his wake, and some among them are barbarians looking to redeem their names in a new land.

Bards

As a cultural wellspring, Seorsus—and in particular the Central Cities—holds performance and entertainment in high esteem and demand. One of the central cities, Oblectant, is completely dedicated to the betterment and enjoyment of the arts, and any performer can find opportunity there. Manifesting from frenetic street festivals, to the studious halls of Bell University, to the crystalline elegance of the Dragon’s Teeth amphitheater, the passions of Oblectant’s citizens know no limits. This intensity has even produced a saying known throughout the nation: no great performer will forever die so long as there is music in Oblectant.

Even outside of Oblectant, a performer has many opportunities to excel. Politicians, storeowners, and merchant guilds are all keenly aware of the impact a charismatic face can have, and many seek the services of skilled spokesmen. Travelers, far from their homes, find comfort in local song and art even in a land so distant. And a rural life may become grim indeed without wanderers who tell tales or sing songs of grand excitement and adventure.

Clerics

Although outwardly Seorsus may seem thoroughly secular and unaffiliated, its history and government are steeped in religion. The temples of Abadar, Shelyn, and Cayden Cailean hold particularly strong political influence, and many of their clerics become officiaries in the grand courts of Regunt. But for every cleric who wields the might of cities, there are a thousand others dedicated to more personal endeavors.

Clerics of many faiths, but especially of Sarenrae, Pharasma, and Erastil, roam the cities and countryside performing good works and aiding public services. Some heed a call to protect their loved ones, and join the National Police Force—a calling often heard by the faithful of Iomedae. Others still seek scholarship in the Knight Library or Bell University, or to study the mysterious and powerful relic shrines that dot the country.

Druids

The bounteous lands of the valley of Multae are full of marvelous phenonmena and natural resources, and there is great need for those who would steward those resources correctly. As a result, the druidic population of Seorsus is not just composed of local druids, but also of those who have traveled long distances to experience the wonders of the valley. In particular, the nation has a considerable influx of druids from Alberi—even those of the reclusive Bestial Tribes. Once in the country, druids tend to pursue their own goals as wanderers, members of a grove, or even as members of larger organizations for—or perhaps against—the government.

Groves are groups of druids that typially gather together at sites of particular spiritual significance. There, they tend to flora and fauna of the area and ensure that the site is preserved against any who would do it harm. The Three Bridge Falls Druids are especially well known to locals and tourists for their protection of the Three Bridge Falls area, and are perhaps more famous than the druids would like.

Druids that are members of larger organizations tend to work with a temple or the government. There they perform local functions such as ensuring that harbors and rivers are maintained, and giving advance warning for weather. They may also perform environmental advisories and studies for government projects. Not all organizations are so friendly towards the smooth operation of local and national government, however. One particularly infamous group, known as the Balancers, are publicly a pro-nature lobbying group and they frequently clash with other agencies on matters of environmental policy.

Fighters

Martial prowess is a much sought after skill in Seorsus, and the gifted will find many opportunities to display their strengths. Although there is no formal army, the National Police Force is always looking for those of sharp mind and strong arm to help keep the peace. Because of their limited manpower, the police have learned to fight smart, and they employ a wide range of skilled combat researchers and engineers to develop new tactics and equipment. Fighters looking to earn more than the govnerment is willing to pay might prefer to take on the role of bodyguard or strongman for a wealthy noble or shop owner. Others still may prefer to take a riskier path and bring their feats to the stage as combat performers —masquerading their deadly arts as performance pieces or sport in the Lavender Fields of Oblectant. Outside the Central Cities, those skilled in combat and with good knowledge of the land excel as hunters or guards for villages and trade caravans.

Monks

There is only one official monk sect in the nation, the Monks of the Empty Cup. They are a sect sworn to poverty and truth. In addition to the good works they do for the communities around the country, they also act as a counter-lobby to the economic elite of Seorsus. Although there is only one official sect, many of the librarians in the Knight Library are monks who worship Irori. Regardless of affiliation, martial skill and spiritual leadership are valued traits all around the nation. Whether as enforcers for the National Police Force, covert bodyguards, performers, or guides for those who wander from the path to enlightenment, a monk will not find herself without a purpose for long.

Paladins

Although the nation was founded as the result of a crusade, its economic and political success have made the environment less than welcoming for holy knights. Many government and mercantile leaders seen paladins as unpredictable and potentially disruptive zealots who might damage the smooth functioning of the nation. Even among the temples represented in the Senate, most choose to send their paladins to other parts of the world. However, there are many causes worthy of advancement in Seorsus, and those who can adhere to more tolerant or flexible codes frequently find their efforts rewarded. In particular, paladins of Sarenrae and Erastil are often welcomed with open arms for their attempts to be understanding and mend the sufferings of the people. Some members of the National Police Force are also paladins of Iomedae, and they snuff out crime and corruption with zeal—though always under the careful guidance of their superiors.

But despite the best efforts of the nation’s leadership to keep the zealots away, they are not always so successful. There are occasional instances of activism from both clandestine and public groups of divine warriors who vie for greater recognition and political influence.

Rangers

Although the valley of Multae is relatively small and geographially isolated, much of its lands have yet to be tamed and its mines have yet to be fully charted. The wilds can be dangerous, and as the people of Seorsus forge ever outwards and downwards they are in need of those who can provide guidance through unknown perils. Explorers and hunters ply the forests and mountains for quarry of bear, wolves, mountain lions, or perhaps an Aurumvorax caught feasting on a surface ore vein. The brave or foolhardy willing to delve deep into caverns on behalf of mining operations may find foes stranger still, but great bounty awaits those who fulfill their duties.

In the parts of the country fresh with civilization, seasoned woodsmen and outland police patrols protect fledgling villages and secure survival from season to season. Meanwhile in the heart of the country, those with connections to the wilds can deal in lucrative exotic pelts, trophies, and other goods. For those who do not wish to exploit their talents for profit from slaughter there are many men, women, and children who will pay handsomly to see talented animals and their trainers perform in Oblectant.

Rogues

Although the Central Cities are lawful and safe places, a rogue’s talents are nonetheless very useful. Merchants and politicians alike can always find a role for someone with quick hands and silent feet, and in a land of tourism and commerce pickpockets and con artists have ample choice of targets if they’re willing to take the risk. Some reformed scoundrels might choose to redeem themselves as bounty hunters or undercover agents for the National Police Force. Others instead opt to live as performers—bringing joy and excitement instead of aggravation and ruin. The Silver Hilt Troupe is particularly well known for taking in those of dubious pasts and repurposing them to more gainful employment as skillful daredevils.

Outside of the Central Cities, rural life provides fewer opportunities for a rogue, and those who cannot or will not make livings as trappers or hunters often turn to criminal undertakings.

Sorcerers

Opportunities of all kinds can appear unexpectedly in Seorsus, and a sorcerer’s ability to instanly access the full wealth of her magical strengths is an invaluable tool for responding to sudden windfalls. In the bustling Central Cities, sorcerers are valued as enchanters in crafting workshops, but they are especially valued in Oblectant. There, the constantly changing festivals and performances often demand immediate response to crazy schemes and outlandish requests. A company of sorcerers known as Light the Sky! Fireworks and other Visual Effects! has specialized in this field, and keeps a sizable retinue of magical adepts on retainer for hire by performers in need.

A sorcerer’s natural force of personality also benefits those who choose to employ their powers to further some goal beyond wealth and entertainment. Many sorcerers of celestial and infernal bloodlines take leadership positions in local government and mercantile operations, where they excel in bringing allies and adversaries under their sway. Others still choose to unleash the full extent of their potential as members of the National Police Force.

Wizards

The vast libraries and combined cultural knowledge of the Central Cities are a great resource to many wizards, and none will struggle to find employment here. The government of Seorsus employs a huge number of wizards in the Department of Customs and Immigration, where they research and approve requests for transit using the country’s magical gates. This approval process also involves the charging of keys for the gates and their tranmission to the proper recipients. In exchange for their services and loyalties, the Department of Customs and Immigration funds research grants for these wizards and also provides shelter and workspaces.

Craftsman also value skilled wizards, and many vie to employ the best in their workshops. For those who cannot afford a full time enchanter, supply shops offer the end results of the creative process. Berthold’s Magic Supply is a particularly well known name in Crent, and it employs skilled enchanters to fulfill bulk and custom requests for their clients.

Character Traits

Character traits are short summaries about your character’s history, motivations, or personality. They help you provide a few quick blurbs about aspects of your character and reward you with mechanical benefits for defining these concepts. Traits also serve to help other players and the GM understand how all the characters might integrate as a party.

You may choose two character traits at the start of the scenario. You may not choose the same trait twice. Combat, Equipment, Faith, Magic, Race, Regional, Religion, and Social traits are not listed in this guide and should be taken from other material if you wish to use them. This guide defines only Campaign traits.

In addition to your character traits, your party should also work together to establish preexisting connections between each character and at least one other party member. Try to spread out these connections as much as possible between the members of the group. This will give the group cohesion and reason for sticking together rather than working alone. Remember, you can always ask your GM for assistance with establishing traits and bonds between characters.

Campaign Traits

Campaign traits are character traits that are guaranteed to be integrated with some part of the scenario’s storyline. They provide you with a surefire way to tie your character into the story and seed your thoughts with ideas for character motivation and history. To accomplish this, they are much more prescriptive than other types of traits. You do not have to choose a campaign trait as one of your two character traits, but if you don’t you should discuss your character with your GM so that she can plan out how to involve your character with the story.

It bears repeating that no matter what traits you choose, you should always strive to establish a pre-existing connection with at least one other character in the party before play begins. Campaign traits are a fast and effective way to do this, but are not the only way.

Attacked by Gnolls

You were attacked by a band of gnolls recently in your life, and now you harbor a deep, burning hatred for the monsters. Perhaps your village was ransacked, and though you were not killed—due to either resourcefulness or blind luck—many of your friends and loved ones were. Or perhaps your caravan was attacked on the road and you lost your belongings or close companions to the gnollish bandits. Whatever the situation, you’ve had something dear stripped from you, and your dreams are haunted by cackles and barks in the night. You’ve sworn to yourself that no gnoll will escape from you alive from this day on, and you’ve taken to studying them with malicious fervor.

You may choose one of the following trait bonuses:

  • You receive a +2 trait bonus to Perception checks made to keep watch.

  • Add Abyssal (cannot speak) and Gnollish to your list of languages.

Shadows of the Past

Someone, or multiple people, dear to you have been forcibly taken away before your eyes by sinister figures in dark cloaks. They may have come during the night and burst into your home, or their actions may have taken place in a secluded area in broad daylight. Wherever you were, they were very careful to prevent you from disrupting them rather than kill you or abduct you as well. The police have found no leads for your claim, and since that moment, you have not seen nor heard of those who were taken. You haven’t given up, though, and recently you have turned up rumors that others have gone missing as well.

You may choose one of the following trait bonuses:

  • You receive a +1 trait bonus to all saving throws against fear spells and effects when an ally within 30 feet is threatened.

  • Pick one of the following skills: Knowledge (local), Diplomacy, Intimidate, Disguise, or Sense Motive. You receive a +1 trait bonus to that skill, and that skill is considered a class skill for you.

Driven Out of Business

You once had a profitable enterprise, but were driven out of business by a shady competitor. Your competitor sprang out of nowhere, and his business was unusually well equipped. Word of it spread surprisingly quickly, and at the same time so did rumors which tarnished your reputation. Your suppliers became less and less reliable, and before long, even your most loyal customers had stopped patronizing your business. The damage dealt to your image was so severe that you have been unable to find any way to start again in the commercial world. You’re certain that this sleazy rival was involved in the destruction of your fortune, but you haven’t found any way to prove it yet.

You may choose one of the following trait bonuses:

  • Pick either charms or compulsions. You receive a +1 trait bonus to all saving throws against spells and effects of that type while you and your allies are not in combat.

  • Pick one of the following skills: Bluff, Disable Device, Linguistics, Sense Motive, or Sleight of Hand. You receive a +1 trait bonus to that skill, and that skill is considered a class skill for you.

Alberi

A dense forest nation in southeastern Norolba. Its native population is primarily elven.

Auditioners

A select group of judges who hold auditions and act as gatekeepers for performances at the Dragon’s Teeth.

Balancers

A pro-nature lobbying group which operates in Seorsus.

Berthold’s Magic Supply

The largest and most well-known magic supply shop in Crent.

Bestial Tribes

A collection of animalistic druids groves hailing from Alberi.

Clearlake

The largest lake in the valley of Multae. It is fed by rivers from the Devil’s Tail Mountains and drains underground into the Great Rift.

Crent

Seorsus’s largest trade city.

Department of Customs and Immigration

The government agency responsible for Seorsus’s commerce and immigration policies.

Department of Labor

The government agency responsible for managing the interests and concerns of employers and their employees in Seorsus.

Devil’s Tail Mountains

The mountain range extending out from Galt Uul to the eastern shore of Norolba.

District Representatives

Seorsus government officials that represent a particular district or region in the nation. They are democratically elected by the citizens of their districts.

Dragon’s Neck Mountains

The mountain range which begins at Galt Uul and extends along the western coast of Norolba, through Orgiluud.

Dragon’s Teeth

A grand amethyst amphitheater in Oblectant, and the city’s most prestigious venue.

Dwarfmother

The ancient and powerful queen and root ancestral mother of the dwarves in Orgiluud.

Festival Planning Committee

The government agency responsible for approving and planning public celebrations in Oblectant.

Folda

The northernmost nation in Norolba, and the second largest nation behind Mughim. It specializes in pastoral enterprises and colonial expansion.

Founders

A sect of Abadarans and the original founders of Seorsus. They are now dedicated to spreading the glory of the nation.

Galt Uul

A dormant mega-volcano situated at the intersection of the Devil’s Tail Mountains and the Dragon’s Neck Mountains

Great Rift

A massive fissure that separates Norolba and Mitasur. It is of unknown depth and surrounded by a large cliff wall.

Hariq

Humans from Mughim who form the majority of the kingdom’s native population. Their ancient lineage is said to be touched by fire.

Knight Library

The largest, most complete library in Seorsus, located in the nation’s capital, Regunt.

Krig

A craggy, barren nation in the northeast of Norolba known for its intensely militaristic population.

Light the Sky! Fireworks and other Visual Effects!

A company of magicians in Oblectant specializing in fantastic visual performances and props.

Limen River, Limen

A large river which descends from the Devil’s Tail Mountains and flows southward into the Clearlake.

Mitasur

The southern subcontinent of the landmass torn asunder into by the Great Rift.

Monks of the Empty Cup

A monastic sect in Seorsus sworn to poverty, truth, and charitable work.

Mughim

A massive desert kingdom centered in Norolba.

Multae

A lush valley that lies between the Great Rift and the Devil’s Tail Mountains.

National Police Force

A government-controlled peacekeeping force in Seorsus.

Norolba

The northern subcontinent of the landmass torn asunder into by the Great Rift.

Oblectant

One of the largest cities in Seorsus. It’s residents and industries specialize in the arts and entertainment.

Orgiluud

A primarily dwarven nation which encompasses the majority of the Dragon’s Neck Mountains and the surrounding foothills.

Public Works Agency

The government agency responsible for managing infrastructure and other public resources in Seorsus.

Regunt

The capital of Seorsus, best known for its legal enterprises.

Seorsus

A thriving nation in the valley of Multae. The nation is a cultural melting pot and industrial powerhouse.

Silver Hilt Troupe

A famous band of daredevil performers in Oblectant.

Stewards

Custodians, administrators, and advocates of the Knight Library in Regunt.

Three Bridge Falls

An impressive waterfall and its surrounding landscape in Seorsus.

Three Bridge Falls Druids

A grove of druids who have taken up a mantle of protecting Three Bridge Falls.

Thresholds

Magical gates that can take travelers to and from Seorsus.

Vuori

Tribal humans who live in the highest altitudes of the Devil’s Tail Mountains and the Dragon’s Neck Mountains.
